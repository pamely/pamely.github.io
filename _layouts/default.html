We are exploring efficient methods to diagnose Birth Asphyxia using babies‚Äô cries and federated learning.

I received my Master's degree in Advanced Electrical and Computer Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University where I had the privilege of being supervised by Professor David Vernon for my MSc graduate project. Additionally, I served as his research assistant for the CSSR4Africa project and as his teaching assistant for his courses 18-799-L Robotics: Principles and Practice and 18-799-R Cognitive Robotics. I completed my Bachelor's degree in Software Engineering at the University of Abomey-Calavi (UAC) in 2019.

Research interests

My goal is to design human-centered AI systems that can interact effectively with humans and collaborate with them. At the core of my research interests lies society: not only fostering respect for every individual but also implementing systems that ensure and safeguard individual security.

I am interested in social robotics, optimization, edge computing, deep reinforcement learning, computer vision, and privacy-preserving machine learning/AI.
